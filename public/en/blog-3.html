Visa and Entry Processes: The New Skilled Immigration Act

Back to Overview

In the past, the biggest hurdle in international recruiting was often German bureaucracy. However, with the modernized Skilled Immigration Act, the federal government has introduced significant simplifications to significantly accelerate and ease the influx of qualified workers from non-EU countries.

The Opportunity Card (Chancenkarte)

A centerpiece of the new regulations is the Opportunity Card, which is based on a points system. It allows skilled workers from third countries to enter Germany to look for work. Points are awarded for professional qualifications, professional experience, language skills (German or English), and age, among other things. This is a paradigm shift: candidates no longer necessarily have to present a signed employment contract before they are allowed to enter the country.

The EU Blue Card in Detail

The EU Blue Card remains the most attractive instrument for academics. Under certain conditions, IT specialists can even receive a Blue Card without a formal university degree, provided they can prove sufficient professional experience.

Recognition Partnerships

Another important innovation is the so-called recognition partnership. This allows employers to hire skilled workers while the formal recognition process of their foreign professional qualification is still ongoing in Germany. In return, the employee commits to carrying out the recognition procedure quickly in parallel with their employment.

Key Benefits for Employers:

  • Significantly shorter waiting times due to the accelerated skilled worker procedure.
  • More flexibility in the recognition of foreign degrees.
  • Direct hiring of IT specialists possible even without a university degree.

Accelerated Skilled Worker Procedure

Companies can initiate an accelerated skilled worker procedure for their future employees with the immigration authority. This bundles responsibilities and significantly shortens the processing time for the visa application.
