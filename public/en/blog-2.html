Attracting Professionals from Abroad

Back to Overview

As the domestic labor market increasingly dries up, international recruiting is becoming a crucial success factor for German companies. However, recruiting skilled workers from abroad brings specific challenges and immense opportunities that must be addressed strategically.

Why Looking Beyond Borders is Essential

Global talent acquisition enables companies to specifically target the exact skills that are lacking locally. Beyond merely filling vacancies, international employees enrich teams with new perspectives, intercultural competencies, and innovative approaches to problem-solving. Diverse teams regularly prove to be more adaptable and better performing in studies.

Strategies for Successful International Recruiting

Effective international recruiting requires more than just publishing English-language job ads. It begins with strong, internationally oriented employer branding. Companies must communicate why they are attractive employers for professionals from around the world. This includes highlighting career opportunities, relocation support, and an inclusive corporate culture.

Using the Right Channels

Potential candidates should be addressed via internationally established platforms (like LinkedIn), specialized job portals, and by collaborating with experienced recruiting agencies. OpenRise provides the ideal network and AI-supported tools to conduct matching efficiently and precisely.

Best Practices for Global Recruiting:

  • Transparent communication regarding relocation support.
  • Streamlined and multilingual application processes.
  • Empathetic handling of video interviews across time zones.

Onboarding: The Key to Long-term Retention

The real work begins after the contract is signed. Excellent relocation management and structured, culturally sensitive onboarding are essential for international employees to quickly settle into the company and in Germany. Investing here drastically reduces turnover and ensures the sustainable success of your HR strategy.
