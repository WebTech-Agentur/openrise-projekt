Are Foreign Workers Qualified?

Back to Overview

A common reservation among German employers regarding international recruitment concerns the quality of education. The question "Can foreign skilled workers meet our standards?" is valid, but in practice, it is often wrongly answered in the negative. Global educational levels have massively converged over the past few decades.

Comparing International Educational Standards

Through the Bologna Process, academic degrees within Europe are standardized and comparable. Bachelor's and Master's degrees from Spain or Poland correspond to their German counterparts. However, in third countries such as India, Brazil, or Iran, there are also excellent universities with rigorous curricula, particularly in engineering and computer science. Many of these graduates bring solid theoretical knowledge that absolutely meets German requirements.

Practical Experience vs. Formal Degrees

In Germany, there is historically a strong focus on formal certificates. In the international environment, especially in the IT sector in countries like the USA or India, proven practical experience and a portfolio often count more than a final certificate. German companies are increasingly learning to adopt this competency-based assessment.

The German Vocational Training System as a Special Case

The German dual vocational training system is almost unique worldwide. Therefore, it is unrealistic to find skilled workers abroad who have undergone exactly the same training as a German "Mechatronics Technician." Instead, you will often find candidates with a Bachelor's degree who take on practical tasks, or people who have perfected their craft through years of hands-on work ("training on the job").

Myths and Facts about Foreign Qualifications:

  • Myth: Lack of German certificates means lack of professional competence.
  • Fact: International professionals often solve problems more pragmatically and innovatively.
  • Fact: Many top universities for STEM subjects are now located in Asia and South America.

Soft Skills and Adaptability

In addition to professional suitability, international talents often bring strong soft skills. Anyone who has the courage to leave their home country and start fresh in a foreign culture demonstrates a high degree of resilience, flexibility, and problem-solving ability. In a modern, agile work environment, these characteristics are at least as valuable as purely technical knowledge.
