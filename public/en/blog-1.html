The Status Quo: Skilled Worker Shortage in Figures

Back to Overview

The shortage of skilled workers in Germany has evolved from a theoretical future scenario into a tangible reality for businesses across all sectors. A detailed look at current statistics reveals the scale of the challenge and underscores the urgent need for action in the German economy.

The Current Situation on the Labor Market

Recent surveys by the German Economic Institute (IW) and the Competence Center for Securing Skilled Labor (KOFA) show that hundreds of thousands of positions remain unfilled across industries. The situation is particularly critical in healthcare, skilled trades, and STEM professions (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics). On average, it currently takes over 150 days to fill an open vacancy in these bottleneck professions.

Focus on STEM and IT Professions

Digitalization is driving the demand for IT experts to a record high. The industry association Bitkom recently reported over 149,000 unfilled positions for IT specialists. This shortage significantly slows down the digital transformation and thus the competitiveness of German companies in the global market.

Key Facts on the Skilled Worker Shortage in 2026:

  • Over 1.5 million unfilled positions across all sectors.
  • Record vacancy times for filling positions.
  • Billions lost in gross value added due to staff shortages.

Demographic Change as the Main Driver

The causes of this development are complex, but the primary driver is demographic change. The baby boomer generation is increasingly entering retirement. By 2035, the German labor market will lack an estimated additional 7 million workers unless there is massive counteraction through immigration. Domestic potential—such as increasing the labor force participation of women or older people—is mathematically insufficient to close this gap.

Solutions for the Future

To remain economically successful, companies must rethink their approach. A proactive, global recruiting strategy is no longer just an option, but a necessity. Attracting international talent offers the greatest leverage to sustainably meet personnel needs while simultaneously bringing new impulses and innovations into the company.
