Finding Qualified Employees in Times of Talent Shortage

Back to Overview

When classic job advertisements remain unanswered and the local market is exhausted, companies must explore new avenues in recruiting. Today, finding qualified employees requires proactivity, creativity, and excellent positioning as an employer—especially on the international stage.

Active Sourcing and Social Networks

Waiting for applications ("Post & Pray") no longer works. Active sourcing, the direct and proactive targeting of candidates, is now the standard in recruitment. Platforms like LinkedIn or specialized networks like GitHub for developers are gold mines for recruiters. The key is a personalized, appreciative approach that stands out from the standardized messages of the competition.

Collaboration with Specialized Agencies

Searching abroad is complex. Language barriers, lack of networks, and cultural differences complicate recruiting. This is where specialized agencies like OpenRise come into play. Through data-driven matching and an established global network, they take over not only the sourcing but also the pre-qualification of candidates. This ensures companies only receive dossiers of applicants who fit professionally and show a genuine motivation to change jobs.

The Role of Corporate Culture

Today, candidates look for more than just a good salary. Purpose, flexible working models (remote work), and an open corporate culture are decisive competitive advantages. Companies must communicate their culture externally in a transparent and authentic way (Employer Branding).

Checklist for Modern Recruiting:

  • Create a clearly defined profile of requirements.
  • Ensure fast and transparent communication with applicants.
  • Actively utilize international platforms.

Optimizing the Candidate Journey

The application process is often a talent's first intensive contact with the company. Complex forms and weeks of waiting for feedback deter top candidates. A lean, digital process with rapid feedback and clear next steps is essential to avoid losing highly sought-after professionals to the competition.
