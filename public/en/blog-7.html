What Are the Costs of Recruiting from Abroad?

Back to Overview

The prejudice that international recruitment is extremely expensive and only affordable for large corporations persists. However, if we take a data-driven look at the true costs, it quickly becomes clear: an open, unfilled position (Cost of Vacancy) is usually significantly more costly for a medium-sized company than the structured acquisition of a talent from abroad.

Direct Recruiting Costs

The most obvious costs include publishing international job advertisements, active sourcing, and possibly the fees for headhunters or placement agencies like OpenRise. Agency fees vary widely but generally range between 15% and 30% of the targeted gross annual salary. In return, these service providers take over the entire risk management of the search.

Hidden Costs: Visas, Relocation, and Onboarding

In addition to pure recruiting, there are often costs associated with the visa process. These include notarizations, translations, and fees at the immigration authority. Furthermore, as an incentive, many employers cover flight costs or pay for a temporary apartment for the first few weeks in Germany (Relocation Package). Realistically, between 2,000 and 5,000 euros should be budgeted per candidate for this.

Opportunity Costs of Unfilled Positions

This point is often overlooked: What does it cost the company if an IT specialist position remains unfilled for six months? Projects are delayed, innovations stagnate, and the existing team suffers from overload. Studies show that an unfilled STEM position costs a company an average of over 400 euros per day in lost value creation.

Cost-Benefit Analysis (ROI):

  • Investment: Recruitment, visa, relocation, and intercultural onboarding.
  • Value Equivalent: High employee loyalty, closing critical gaps, avoiding project delays.
  • Conclusion: The ROI of a successful international hire is usually positive after 6 to 9 months.

Long-term Profitability

Statistically speaking, international professionals who have been successfully integrated with the employer's support show above-average loyalty and a lower turnover rate. The initial additional costs for relocation thus quickly amortize through a longer tenure in the company.
