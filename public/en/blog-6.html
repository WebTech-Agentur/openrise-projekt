How to Determine if International Professionals Meet German Qualifications?

Back to Overview

Evaluating foreign professional qualifications is often the bottleneck in international recruiting. Employers face the challenge of classifying certificates in foreign languages and comparing them with the complex requirements of their own job profiles. Fortunately, there are now standardized procedures and proven best practices to simplify this process.

The Formal Recognition Process

The most important point of contact for evaluating foreign university degrees in Germany is the Central Office for Foreign Education (ZAB). It maintains the 'anabin' database, which employers and authorities can use to check whether a foreign university and the degree obtained there are recognized in Germany. For regulated professions (e.g., doctors, teachers), a formal recognition procedure is mandatory; for non-regulated professions, it is often helpful for obtaining a visa.

Correctly Interpreting References and Testimonials

The German concept of detailed qualified reference letters is largely unknown abroad. In Anglo-American or Asian countries, short letters of recommendation or direct reference calls are common instead. Employers should therefore show flexibility and be prepared to contact the candidate's former supervisors directly to get a sense of their performance and work ethic.

Technical Tests and Probationary Work

Since certificates alone do not always tell the whole truth, modern companies rely on competency-based evaluations. For developers, this might be a coding test or a pair programming session. For engineers, case studies or technical discussions are suitable. These practical tests overcome cultural hurdles in self-presentation in a resume.

Checklist for Competency Checking:

  • Use the 'anabin' database for preliminary checking of degrees.
  • Schedule direct reference calls.
  • Conduct standardized, practical work samples (assessments).

Digital Assessments in Recruiting

With the help of modern platforms like OpenRise, the verification of qualifications can be partially automated. Standardized technical assessments and AI-supported validation of uploaded documents drastically minimize the risk of bad hires while simultaneously reducing the administrative burden on the HR department.
